## What is eToro?

eToro is a multi-asset platform. Depending on your country and account settings, you may be able to invest in different asset classes and/or trade CFDs. Always check what applies to you on the provider’s official website.

## Who it’s for

- Beginners who want a single interface to explore markets.
- Users interested in social features (when available) and learning from public portfolios.

## What to verify first (before you deposit or place a trade)

The most important thing with any “multi-asset” platform is understanding **what you are actually trading**:

- Is it the underlying asset (e.g., investing) or a **CFD** (derivative)?
- Is **leverage** enabled by default or optional?
- Are there **overnight/financing fees** (common with leveraged products)?
- What are the **fees/spreads** for your instrument?

If you can’t answer these questions with confidence, start smaller and slow down.

### Asset vs CFD: a simple rule

- If you see **CFD**, **leverage**, or **overnight/financing fees**, you’re likely dealing with a higher-risk product.
- If you can’t explain “what I’m buying” in one sentence, you’re not ready to scale position size.

Speed is not the goal at the beginning. Clarity is.

## Copy features

Copy features can be educational, but they can also create a false sense of safety. A practical approach:

- Prefer diversified approaches over “one hero trader”.
- Look beyond returns: check drawdowns, consistency, number of positions, and how risk is managed.
- Start with a small allocation and review behavior during bad periods (that’s when risk shows up).

Copying doesn’t remove risk; it can amplify it if you chase performance.

Practical checklist before you copy (when available):

- has the profile lived through bad markets (and how)?
- maximum drawdown: can you emotionally tolerate it?
- concentration: many correlated positions = hidden risk
- leverage/complex products: avoid early on

## Fees and friction

Exact fees vary by product and region. Typical items to check on any platform:

- spreads and commissions
- currency conversion (FX) if you deposit/trade in different currencies
- withdrawal fees and minimums
- inactivity fees (if applicable)

Fees that look small can become significant when you trade often or with leverage.

## Limitations & things to verify

- Fees, spreads, and conditions vary by product, region, and market conditions.
- Some instruments may be CFDs: understand leverage and liquidation risk.
- Copy features (when available) are not a guarantee of results and can increase behavioral risk.

## Practical use cases

- **Learning:** follow markets with small sizes to build good habits.
- **Long-term investing:** focus on diversification and position sizing.
- **Avoiding leverage:** if you are new, understand the product type before trading.

## Common mistakes

- Thinking “multi-asset” means “low risk”: risk depends on product type (asset vs CFD) and leverage.
- Overtrading because the UI makes action feel effortless.
- Copying returns instead of copying a process (risk rules, sizing, discipline).
- Ignoring total costs (spreads, FX, withdrawals) until it’s too late.

## Quick test (small amount)

Before you scale deposits:

1. Enable 2FA and secure your email.
2. Make a **small deposit** in your currency and note total costs (including FX).
3. On one instrument, confirm: product type, leverage, risk warnings, and fees.
4. If possible, do a **small withdrawal** to validate the process.

This “process test” often beats hours of reading.

## Checklist

- Is eToro available in my country for the products I want?
- Do I understand the product type (asset vs CFD) and the role of leverage?
- Have I reviewed current fees/spreads and non-trading fees (FX, withdrawals, inactivity)?
- Do I have a risk rule (max loss, position sizing, no-leverage policy if beginner)?
- If I use copy features: have I checked drawdowns and strategy behavior in bad markets?
