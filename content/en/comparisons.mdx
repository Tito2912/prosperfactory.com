Our comparisons are written to help you make a **clear, risk-aware decision**.

## How to read a comparison

A useful comparison doesn’t say “A is better than B”. It helps you answer:

- *What am I actually buying?* (asset vs CFD/derivative)
- *What’s the total cost?* (fees, spreads, FX, withdrawals, inactivity)
- *Where does friction show up?* (withdrawals, verification, support)
- *Can I follow this setup for 90 days?* (simplicity)

If you’re unsure, start with the [platform checklist](/guides/how-to-choose-a-platform/).

## The criteria we check

Most decisions come down to six repeatable checks:

1. **Role**: analysis (charts/alerts) vs execution (orders).
2. **Product type**: asset vs CFDs/derivatives, leverage involvement.
3. **End-to-end costs**: not one fee—full lifecycle costs.
4. **Withdrawals & access**: timelines, minimums, support.
5. **Security & custody**: 2FA, email security, custody tradeoffs.
6. **Complexity**: complexity increases mistakes and risk.
